Establishing a singular web point of access for Tesla service documentation.

The User.

Third Party Partners, Tesla Technicians, and Car Owners.

The Endpoint.

Consumer Responsive Web.

The Tools.

Figma.

The Role.

Lead Product Designer.

The Problem.

We were at risk of getting sued. Due to recent law changes, it was imperative that we provided easy-to-access public repair information to users in a short amount of time. This need brought up the question...

How can we provide service information to customers, partners, and third-party shops in a single holistic portal?

Lots of different users searching for service information.
Third Party Body Shop Partner
Local Auto Shop Owner
Tesla Owner
Each of these users have different but often overlapping goals...
Requiring users to remember different sites for similar needs...
Which birthed a new vision.

A singular web portal that would serve as the point of access for all service related functions.

Leading design for the one-stop mobile app for Tesla Service technicians.

The User.

Mobile, Service Center, and Pre-Delivery Technicians.

The Endpoint.

Enterprise Native App.

The Tools.

Figma.

The Role.

Lead Product Designer.

The Problem.

An Ancient App for Technicians. A small app that previously had a specific purpose and functionality had ballooned into a massive application with more personas and functionality Frankenstein-ed on top of it. This led to high training costs, cumbersome flows, and higher costs of labor.

How can we provide a one-stop shop for each persona, while also streamlining workflows while reducing costs of training and time to complete tasks?

Lots of primary users on Ranger.
Mobile Vehicle Technician
Energy Technician
Service Center Advisor
Pre-Delivery Technician
Each had specific needs and purposes which was added into a singular experience.
Resulting in a high complicated information architecture (IA). I identified cleaning up this IA as my main goal in the redesign.
Working with users, I found the key differences as well as how important each piece of information was to them.

All this resulted in a new technician experience that was more context-aware, prioritized user needs, and tailored the experience to their persona.

Revolutioning the parts purchasing experience.

The User.

Service Center Technicians, Parts Advisors, and General Public.

The Endpoint.

Consumer and Enterprise Responsive Web.

The Tools.

Figma.

The Role.

Lead Product Designer.

The Problem.

Parts were disconnected and inaccessible. Specifications about parts were spread across multiple applications and it was difficult to find a source of truth for them. Furthermore, due to law changes, we were required make parts available to purchase publicly. This dilemma led to me asking the question...

How can we create an intuitive parts catalog experience which scales with our heavy data?

We have thousands of parts, each organized with high complexity in our backend system.
Competitors have tried to solve this problem of reducing complexity, but not much success.
The current standards were obtuse. Most of this is because parts are primarily identified through a diagram.
This was the biggest challenge. How would we intuitively guide the user to their desired part...

The solution. A shopping experience guided by hero pages, entry flows, and interactive diagrams.
